About

Open Congress brings together public information from the Peruvian Congress, making it easier to explore bills, congress members, political groups, and legislative activity in one place.
This project began as a side project and was later formalized by a team of six students from Computer Science and the Harris School of Public Policy at the University of Chicago.
Its goal is to improve transparency and support the development of civic technology in service of democracy, accountability, and public participation.

The Congress

The Peruvian Congress is one of the three branches of the Peruvian state, alongside the executive and judicial branches. Established as an independent branch under the Constitution, it is responsible for drafting, debating, and approving laws in Peru.
During the 2021-2026 legislative period, Congress operates as a unicameral body with 130 members.
Every law in Peru begins as a bill and must pass through a legislative process before becoming law.
